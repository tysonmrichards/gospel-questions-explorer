How can I know if something is true when I can't see proof?
Like Korihor, many people struggle with believing in things they cannot physically see or prove. This question addresses the relationship between faith, knowledge, and evidence.

Alma's response teaches that demanding physical proof isn't the only way to know truth. Like Professor Taggart shared about dark matter comprising 90% of the universe yet being invisible, some of the most important realities can't be seen but are still real and powerful.

How do I plant faith in my heart when believing feels difficult?
Sometimes faith doesn't come naturally. Alma's seed analogy provides a practical approach for those who struggle with belief but want to experience spiritual growth.

Alma 32:27
"Even if ye can no more than desire to believe, let this desire work in you..."
 Alma 32:28
"Give place, that a seed may be planted in your heart..."
 Alma 32:28 (continued)
"It beginneth to enlarge my soul; yea, it beginneth to enlighten my understanding, yea, it beginneth to be delicious to me."
ðŸ’­ Personal Reflection:

The beautiful thing about Alma's teaching is that you don't have to start with perfect faith. Even just wanting to believe is enough to begin the experiment. I love the SEED acronym: Swell, Enlarge, Enlighten, Delicious - these are the fruits of planting God's word in our hearts.

Where and how should I worship if I can't always be in church?
The poor Zoramites were cast out of their synagogues and wondered how they could worship. This question explores how worship can happen anywhere and at any time.

Alma 33:4-11
Zenos's prayer: "Thou art merciful, O God, for thou hast heard my prayer even when I was in the wilderness..."
 Alma 34:18-27
"Cry unto him in your houses, yea, over all your household... in your fields... in your wilderness..."
 Alma 34:27
"Let your hearts be full, drawn out in prayer unto him continually..."
ðŸ’­ Personal Reflection:

Worship isn't confined to a building or a specific day. As Professor Taggart taught, worship can happen in our wilderness (uncertain places), our fields (daily work), our houses (close relationships), and even our closets (private struggles). True worship transforms how we live toward God and others.

Alma's prayer teaches us that we don't need our suffering to be removed - we need God to be with us as we suffer. When we live in relationship with Christ, our afflictions can be "swallowed up in the joy of Christ." This doesn't mean no pain, but that pain is transformed by divine love.
